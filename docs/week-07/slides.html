The Evolution of Photography

From light-sensitive plates to digital sensors ‚Äî a journey through how images became data.

üìú Early Beginnings (1820s‚Äì1830s)

  • Roots of photography trace back to the camera obscura and experiments with light-sensitive chemicals.
  • In 1826‚Äì1827, Joseph Nic√©phore Ni√©pce created the first successful photograph, the heliograph.
  • Used bitumen-coated pewter plates and required very long exposure times.
  • The first photograph - View from the Window at Le Gras

Technique: ‚ÄúBitumen on glass‚Äù ‚Äî primitive yet revolutionary image capture.

üì∑ Daguerreotype (1839)

  • Louis Daguerre perfected a new process using silver-coated copper plates.
  • Produced highly detailed, clear images with shorter exposure times.
  • First commercially successful photographic method.
  • A video demo of Daguerreotype

Impact: Made personal portraiture possible ‚Äî an early democratization of photography.

üéûÔ∏è Film Roll and Kodak (1888)

  • George Eastman introduced the Kodak camera with roll film.
  • Replaced fragile glass plates with flexible celluloid film.
  • Enabled multiple exposures on a single roll ‚Äî photography became portable and affordable.

Slogan: ‚ÄúYou press the button, we do the rest.‚Äù

üåà Color Photography (1907‚Äì1935)

  • 1907: Lumi√®re brothers introduced Autochrome plates ‚Äî first practical color process.
  • 1935: Kodak launched Kodachrome film, making color widely accessible. A demo video.

  • Color revolutionized journalism, art, and everyday documentation.

üìΩÔ∏è The Golden Age of Film (1930s‚Äì1950s)

  • Advancements in film emulsions and camera design led to rapid growth.
  • Hollywood embraced film for motion pictures; photography became mass culture.
  • Iconic photographers like Ansel Adams and Dorothea Lange shaped documentary and artistic photography.

üíª Digital Shift (1990s‚Äì2000s)

  • Digital sensors replaced chemical film.
  • Images stored as pixel data ‚Äî arrays of numeric values representing light intensity and color. (More on this later)
  • Instant feedback, lower cost, and ease of editing - global adoption!

Impact: Photography became a core digital data source ‚Äî a bridge to computer vision.

üì∏ Revival and Niche Interest (2010s‚ÄìPresent)

  • Film photography experiences a resurgence among artists and enthusiasts.
  • Valued for its aesthetic qualities, grain texture, and tactile process.
  • Companies continue to produce film stock and analog cameras for niche markets.

Trend: Blending analog craft with digital editing and sharing.

üß† From Film to Data

  • Transition from chemical light capture ‚Üí numerical light capture.
  • Modern digital images = 2D matrices of pixel values.

What is a digital image?

  • A 2D array (matrix) of pixel intensity values (more on these values later)
  • Each pixel represents light intensity at a specific spatial location
  • Images can be grayscale or color (multi-channel)

Digital Image Representation - the dimensions

  • Spatial resolution ‚Üí number of pixels (e.g. 1920√ó1080)
  • Bit depth ‚Üí number of bits per pixel (e.g. 8-bit, 24-bit)
  • Color depth ‚Üí how many color channels (1 for grayscale, 3 for RGB)
But what are the numbers in a digital image? What are "pixel intensity values"?
- They are numerical data that describe a single pixel's brightness or color or transparency within a digital image, depending on what color model is used.

RGB Color Model

  • Each pixel = 3 values (Red, Green, Blue)
  • Colors are formed by mixing intensities of each channel
  • Example: (255, 0, 0) ‚Üí pure red

The actual shape of a RGB-colored image: a 3D array ‚Üí (height, width, 3)

RGBA Model

  • Extension of RGB with Alpha channel for transparency
  • (R, G, B, A), where A controls opacity (0=transparent, 255=opaque)

Loading Images in Python


from PIL import Image
img = Image.open("example.jpg")
img.show()
  • Pillow (PIL fork) is great for basic image handling
  • Use numpy.array(img) to convert to a NumPy array

Image as a NumPy Array


import numpy as np
arr = np.array(img)
print(arr.shape, arr.dtype)
Example output: (256, 256, 3) uint8 ‚Üí 256√ó256 pixels, 3 color channels, each number is an 8-bit unsigned integer.

Visualizing Images with Matplotlib


import matplotlib.pyplot as plt

plt.axis('off')

Tip: Matplotlib automatically scales pixel values and handles RGB correctly.
