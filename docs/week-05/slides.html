These are all data!

In fact, they represent different perspectives about data:

- data in phone📱: digital, quantized for easy measurement (e.g. 250GB)

- data in a spreadsheet📋: structured, note that not all data can be put into rows and columns

- data about me on social media🧃: behavioural data, include more than just numbers (e.g. the images I have uploaded)

- data from scientific experiments🔬: natural data, comes from observation

Why are numbers important?

Recap on "data modality"
Data modalities🕶️
Information that we can gather from the world and store in digital systems as "data", are mainly from four modalities:
- 👁️Image (picture, video)
- 📝Text (written language)
- 🎵Audio (music, speech)
- 🧩Tabular(this week's weather in degree celsius, everyone's birthday in this class, sensor data, etc.)
Can you think of any information that is not from the four categories?
🧠 My mind-blowing moment:
- Information from any of those three categories (image, text and audio) can be digitized / numberified aka be represented by just a bunch of numbers.
Data modalities🕶️
- Each data modality has its own characteristics and challenges for representation, processing and analysis.
- We structure our next few lectures based on the different data modalities, each week focusing on one modality (with a few exceptions).
